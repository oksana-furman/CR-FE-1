3 Best Christmas Coffee Recipes!

Christmas is just around the corner and these Christmas coffee recipes will definitely boost your cafés sales! So make sure you reach your full potential during Christmas season and take these recipes to your cafés manu during Christmas season!

CHRISTMAST LATTE

This classic is hard one to beat. I can remember preparing hundred of these gingerbread treats during Christmas season when I was still working behind the bar. Gingerbread flavour just belongs to Christmas and it works really well in a milky smooth latte.

Recipe

  • 30 ml Paulig espresso
  • 1,5 dl steamed milk or plant based beverage
  • 2 cl gingerbread syrup

Prepare a single espresso into a mug. Mix warm espresso shot and syrup together. Lightly froth the milk or plant-based beverage and pour it over the espresso.

CHRISTMAS MOCHA

Feel fancy and are craving for something even sweeter? Then Christmas Mocha is definitely the one for you! This chocolately treat will solve all of your customer's problems and take them straight to the mood of Christmas.

Recipe

  • 30 ml Paulig espresso
  • 1,5 dl steamed milk
  • 2 cl Tazza Chocolate sauce or 1 Tazza stick
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • Gingerbread cream*

Add Tazza Chocolate sauce on the bottom of the glass. Prepare a single espresso into a mug. Lightly froth the dairy milk and pour it over the espresso. Finish with gingerbread cream and garnish with cinnamon and gingerbread cookies.

*Gingerbread whipped cream: Mix 0,5 l of cream and 3 tsp Santa Maria Gingerbread spice in siphon.

CHRISTMAS FILTER COFFEE

Your cafés doesn't have the space for an espresso machine but you would still want to surprise your customer during Christmas season? Then this filter coffee treat is for your café! Cinnamon and cardamom lift up the filter coffee to another dimensions and delicious gingerbread whipped cream finishes the treat.

Recipe

  • 60 grams of coffee
  • 1 litre of water
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Gingerbread whipped cream

Add the spices on top of the ground coffee in the filter cone and prepare the filter coffee as you would normally do. Finish with gingerbread cream and garnish with cinnamon and gingerbread cookies. Just make sure that you washed your coffee pots or thermoses really well to avoid the spices sticking to it.

Gingerbread whipped cream: Mix 0,5 l of cream and 3 tsp Santa Maria Gingerbread spice in siphon.

Iced Coffee

Better-than-Starbucks Coffee Frappé

  • 2 double shots cold espresso
  • 60 ml whole milk
  • 2 tablespoons packed light brown sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 100 g ice cubes
  • Whipped cream, for serving (optional)

Place espresso, milk, sugar, vanilla extract, and ice cubes in a blender. Begin on low speed and increasing to high speed, blend until thick, smooth, and creamy, 20 to 40 seconds.

Pour into a glass and top with whipped cream if desired.

Iced Long Black

  • Two espresso shots
  • Cold tap water
  • Ice cubes

You have a few options with an iced long black. You can either have brewed chilled espresso coffee poured over ice so it is half-coffee and half-iced water, or you can brew the espresso before you go to bed and place it overnight in your fridge.

Then, the following day, you can combine the chilled espresso coffee with cold tap water and pour it over ice.

If you like to sit out in the sun while enjoying your iced coffee, I recommend making a few coffee ice cubes beforehand.

Coffee ice cubes are made with freshly brewed coffee that has been cooled completely before pouring it into an ice cube tray or ice cube bag.

Then, after it's frozen, you can use it to cool your iced coffees, but instead of diluting your iced coffee with water, the coffee ice cubes will slowly release more coffee into your drink!

Affogato

  • 2 scoops vanilla ice cream high quality
  • 1 shot espresso
  • 1 tablespoon nut or coffee liqueur (optional)
  • dark chocolate , for grating on top
  1. Brew an espresso (one per person). Scoop 1-2 scoops of vanilla ice cream into a wide glass or bowl and pour over a shot of espresso.
  2. Pour 1 tablespoon of nocino nut liqueur or your liqueur of choice over the ice cream and grate over a little dark chocolate.

Caramel Fever Freakshake

Salty caramel freakshake with espresso.
  • 2 shots (50-60 ml) of Paulig espresso
  • Nutella (nuss-nugat-crème)
  • Pretzels
  • Flake salt
  • 2 cl caramel sauce
  • 250 ml milk
  • Whipped cream
  1. Garnish the inside of a glass (300 ml) with Nutella.
  2. Add caramel sauce and a pinch of flake salt in the glass.
  3. Prepare a double espresso on top.
  4. Steam milk and pour it into the glass.
  5. Fill the glass with whipped cream and garnish with caramel sauce and a pinch of flake salt.
  6. Add loads of small pretzels on top.
